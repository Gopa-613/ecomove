Our Project

A focused initiative to study and compare the environmental impact of three major vehicle fuel types—Petrol, Diesel, and Electric—with a special emphasis on NOx (Nitrogen Oxides) and CO2 (Carbon Dioxide) emissions. These pollutants are key contributors to poor air quality and climate change.

Through extensive data analysis and research, we have measured how each fuel type affects pollution levels. While petrol and diesel vehicles are known sources of direct tailpipe emissions, including high levels of NOx and CO2, our study also considers the often-overlooked environmental cost of electric vehicles (EVs).

EVs are marketed as "clean" alternatives, and while they produce zero tailpipe emissions, our findings show that their overall environmental footprint isn't negligible. A major concern is the production and disposal of lithium-ion batteries used in EVs. These batteries are energy-intensive to manufacture, emitting significant CO2 during mining, refining, and cell production processes. Additionally, the lack of widespread battery recycling and challenges in safe disposal present growing environmental threats.

In the context of India's rapidly urbanizing transport ecosystem, this comparison is especially critical. Government reports and independent studies (e.g., Drishti IAS, ICCT) highlight that NOx emissions from conventional fuel vehicles are one of the leading causes of declining urban air quality and contribute significantly to the Air Quality Index (AQI).

Our project doesn't aim to promote or criticize a particular fuel type, but to bring awareness through data-backed insights. We provide:

  • Comparative analysis of NOx and CO2 emissions from Petrol, Diesel, and Electric vehicles
  • Correlation between NOx emissions and AQI deterioration across fuel types
  • Yearly trends in CO2 data to visualize the impact of vehicle types on environment
  • Visualization of pollutant trends using interactive dashboards and time-series graphs
  • Machine learning-based predictions of future emission levels and their potential AQI impact

In conclusion, while the adoption of EVs is a positive move toward reducing urban air pollution, it must be backed by sustainable battery manufacturing, recycling infrastructure, and clean energy grids to be truly effective. Our research aims to help users, policymakers, and students make informed decisions toward a cleaner future.
